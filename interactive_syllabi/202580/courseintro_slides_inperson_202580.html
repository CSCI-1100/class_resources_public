What This Course Is All About

🌐 Course Mission

Navigate the digital age confidently and creatively! This course is designed to arm you with essential tech skills to make you an informed and enlightened digital citizen.

🎯 What You'll Master

  • Participate safely and ethically online
  • Evaluate information sources critically
  • Create tech solutions for real problems
  • Communicate effectively about technology

💡 Key Areas

  • Information Literacy
  • Programming Fundamentals
  • Data Literacy & Analysis
  • Cybersecurity & Privacy
  • AI & Machine Learning

🚀 Future Ready

This course offers a broad exploration of today's digital landscape, preparing you for a future where technology is in constant evolution.

What You'll Achieve

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

1

Digital Citizenship

Engage securely and responsibly in digital communities

2

Critical Evaluation

Assess information sources critically for credibility and security risks

3

Solution Design

Design technology-based solutions to real-world problems

4

Tech Communication

Communicate effectively about computing technology

5

Productivity Tools

Apply modern software tools to enhance productivity

6

Emerging Technologies

Explore AI and contemporary digital technologies

Personal Learning Labs

🔬 What Are Personal Learning Labs?

Individual projects aligned with your interests and the week's topics. Whether you're exploring online privacy, building a website, investigating AI, or examining global computer connections - these labs let you apply what you've learned!

📋 Lab Requirements

  • Must be completed  individually
  • No generative AI unless specified
  • One lab per week (except Word/PowerPoint weeks)
  • Can use campus resources like Center for Academic Achievement

📝 Getting Help Guidelines

If you receive assistance, credit must be attributed:

  • Resource: Person/website
  • Assistance Received: What help you got
  • Individual Contribution: What you did with the help

⏰ Lab Deadlines

On-site labs: Complete during lab session or submit by the following lab session
Online labs: Submit by Sunday 11:59 PM EDT

Quizzes & Exit Tickets

📊 Weekly Quizzes

  • Cover weekly lesson materials
  • Two attempts allowed (higher score counts)
  • Multiple choice, true/false, fill-in-blank, matching
  • Due: Sundays 11:59 PM EDT

🎫 Exit Tickets

  • Short, completion-based quiz
  • Gauge your understanding and enjoyment
  • On-site only: Given during lecture
  • Cannot be made up

💡 Success Tips

  • Review all weekly materials before taking quizzes
  • Use your first quiz attempt as a learning opportunity
  • Your feedback on Exit Tickets helps us improve the course
  • No Quiz or Exit Ticket in Week 1!

🎯 Grade Drops

We'll drop your 3 lowest Exit Ticket grades and 1 lowest Quiz grade from your overall grade.

Academic Honesty & AI Use

📜 What's Expected

Follow ETSU's Honor Code and maintain academic integrity. All submitted work must be your own original effort.

🚫 What NOT to Do

  • Plagiarize from any source
  • Use generative AI to complete assignments
  • Submit AI-generated work as your own
  • Reuse your own work without permission

✅ AI Guidelines

  • You'll study AI as a course topic
  • Can use AI as planning aids
  • Must cite all sources properly
  • Focus on learning, not shortcuts

⚖️ Consequences

Academic misconduct can result in reduced grades on assignments, failing the course, and formal misconduct procedures. You have the right to appeal any decisions.
