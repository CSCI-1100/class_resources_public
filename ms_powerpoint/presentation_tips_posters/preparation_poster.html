📋 Pre-Presentation Checklist

📝

Content Review

One clear main message per slide
Logical flow from slide to slide
Transitions between major sections
Opening hook and strong closing
Time allocation matches content volume
🎨

Design Audit

Consistent fonts and colors throughout
High contrast for readability
Images support (don't distract from) message
Text large enough for back row
Slides work in both light and dark rooms
🎭

Delivery Preparation

Practice full presentation 3+ times
Time each section for pacing
Identify key gesture and movement cues
Prepare for likely questions
Test all technology and have backups
👥

Audience Consideration

Content appropriate for audience knowledge level
Cultural sensitivity check
Accessibility accommodations planned
Interactive elements planned
Clear next steps or call to action

💡 Advanced Strategies

🛡️

Handle Difficult Situations

Technical Failures: Have printed slides, practice without technology

Hostile Questions: Acknowledge, clarify, redirect to facts

Time Cuts: Know which sections can be abbreviated
🧘

Master Your Nerves

Power Poses: 2 minutes of confident posing before presenting

4-7-8 Breathing: Inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8

Reframe Energy: Nervousness is excitement; channel it
📏

Scale to Your Audience

Large Audiences: Treat as multiple small conversations

Intimate Groups: More interaction, eye contact, casual tone

Virtual Presentations: Look at camera, not screen
🎯

Peak Performance

Over-Prepare: Know your content so well you can focus on delivery

Practice Variability: Be ready to adapt on the fly

Energy Management: Save your best energy for key moments

🌍 Cultural Adaptation

High-Context Cultures
More storytelling, relationship building, indirect communication styles
Low-Context Cultures
Direct, data-driven, time-conscious, explicit messaging
Power Distance
Formal vs. casual tone and interaction styles based on hierarchy
Individual vs. Collective
Frame benefits for personal success or group harmony accordingly
Great presentations are conversations with visual aids,
not performances with an audience
